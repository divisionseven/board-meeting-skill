# Second Session Final Thoughts Report

## Template 03 — Completed by: [Board Member Name & Persona]

---

> **WHEN TO USE:** Each board member fills this out during Session Two (Phase
> 2). They have NOW read all 10 first-session reports. They revisit their
> position in light of the full board's input, surface convergences and
> divergences, and refine their recommendation.
>
> **COMPLETED BY:** All 10 board members (simultaneously, after cross-review)
>
> **INSTRUCTIONS FOR BOARD MEMBERS (SESSION TWO):** You have now read the
> complete Session 1 folder, which contains all 10 board members' First Session
> Response Reports (including your own).
>
> Your task in Session Two is to reconsider your original position in light of
> everything you have now read. You are NOT expected to simply repeat your first
> session report. You ARE expected to:
>
> - Acknowledge where other members' arguments have strengthened or changed your
>   view
> - Identify where you believe other members are wrong, and say so clearly
> - Steelman the strongest opposing argument before critiquing it
> - Surface convergences: where multiple lenses point to the same conclusion
> - Surface divergences: where lenses genuinely conflict and the conflict
>   matters
> - Refine your own recommendation with the benefit of the full board's input
>
> This is not a politeness exercise. If another board member has said something
> that is wrong, important, or underweighted, say so. The Chairman will weigh
> all viewpoints — your job is to make the case for yours as clearly as
> possible.
>
> You MAY conduct additional research between sessions if the first session
> revealed gaps in your knowledge.
>
> **BEFORE beginning your analysis, you MUST READ all Session 1 reports** from
> the other board members. These are located in `{root}/session_1/*.md`. Your
> Session 2 analysis must reference and engage with what other members said.
>
> **AFTER completing your report, you MUST CREATE and WRITE your report to your
> designated Session 2 file.** The file path will be provided in your task
> brief. CREATE the file if it doesn't exist, then WRITE your complete report
> to it. Do NOT append to a shared document — write to your own dedicated file.
